Reliable Distributed Client-Server System#

Aug. - Dec. 2021, JavaScript

  • The distributed system meets the Distributed Fault-Tolerance Standard.
  • The faults will be injected during the test(replicas will be intentionally crashed) and recovered from (replicas will be intentionally re-launched) while the distributed system is running.
  • We are focused on detecting, and recovering from, two types of fail-silent faults: crash of a single server replica, and loss of a message between a client and the server.
  • What the system contains:
    • Crash faults and message-loss faults
    • Fault detection via heartbeats
    • Active replication with replica consistency
    • Passive replication with replica consistency
    • Checkpointing, logging, duplicate detection
    • Maintenance of group membership
    • Fault injection to observe the system operate under faults
    • Re-introduction of failed replicas to observe how recovery works

Application of Capillary Action Water-soluble Drug Delivery#

Aug. - Dec. 2022, COMSOL

Report: https://angelyuchu02.github.io/18669.pdf

  • This project intends to study and simulate a water-soluble drug working mechanism in the capillary which consists of a permeable membrane on its wall. Due to the capillary action, the drug will dissolve into the water. The system would require high precision as many parameters involved such as concentration, location, time, etc. The model of this process is demonstrated through COMSOL, with a constant flux and inlet velocity of the drug, to study the concentration changes of the drug in the drop over time.

Balloon Inflation in Percutaneous Transluminal Coronary Angioplasty with Various Materials#

Jan. - May. 2023, COMSOL

Report: https://angelyuchu02.github.io/PTCA.pdf

  • This project intends to study and simulate the percutaneous transluminal coronary angioplasty (PTCA) using a balloon catheter to treat coronary stenosis treatment. A plastic tube with an inflatable balloon at one end is inserted to the narrowed coronary artery where plaque had built up. Balloon is inflated multiple times to compress the plaque and widen the artery. Selecting the optimal balloon material in different scenarios is crucial to carry out PTCA. The model will demonstrate the comparison of various materials' mechanical properties such as inflation pressure and hoop stress applied to the balloon when stretched using COMSOL.
